Circular on Issues Concerning Treatment of Enterprise Income Tax in Enterprise Restructuring Business

Cai Shui [2009] No. 59

April 30, 2009

All offices of State Administration of Taxation and local taxation bureaus of all provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities directly under the Central Government, and cities separately designated in the State plan, and Finance Bureau of Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps,

In accordance with Article 20 of the Law of the People's Republic of China on Enterprise Income Tax and Article 75 of the Regulations on the Implementation of the Enterprise Income Tax Law of the People's Republic of China (Order No. 512 of the State Council), issues on the specific treatment of enterprise income tax in connection with enterprise restructuring are notified as follows:

Article 1 For the purpose of this Circular, enterprise restructuring refers to transactions of material change in legal structure or economic structure of an enterprise other than changes in the daily operating activities, including change in legal form of the enterprise, debt restructuring, equity acquisition, asset acquisition, merger, and split.

  1. Change in legal form of an enterprise refers to simple changes in the registered name of the enterprise, address and form of organization of an enterprise organization, excluding other types of restructuring stipulated in this Circular.

  2. Debt restructuring refers to an event in which a creditor grants a concession in accordance with the written agreement entered into between the creditor and the debtor or a written order of a court when the debtor gets into a financial problem.

  3. Equity acquisition refers to a transaction that an enterprise (hereinafter referred as the acquiring enterprise) acquires the equities of another enterprise (hereinafter referred to as the acquired enterprise) to take control over the acquired enterprise. The forms of the consideration payment by the acquiring enterprise include equity consideration, non-equity consideration or a combination of both.

  4. Asset acquisition refers to a transaction that an enterprise (hereinafter referred to as the receiving enterprise) purchases the substantive operating assets of another enterprise (hereinafter referred to as the transferring enterprise). The forms of the consideration payment by the receiving enterprise include equity consideration, non-equity consideration or a combination of both.

  5. Merger refers to the lawful merger of two or more enterprises whereby one or more enterprise (hereinafter referred to as the merged enterprise) transfers all the assets and liabilities to another existing or newly-established enterprise (hereinafter referred to as the merging enterprise) with the shareholders of the merged enterprise receiving equity or non-equity consideration of the merging enterprise.

  6. Split refers to the lawful split of an enterprise whereby an enterprise (hereinafter referred to as the enterprise being split) transfers part or all its assets and liabilities to an existing or newly-established enterprise (hereinafter referred to as the split enterprise) with the shareholders of the enterprise being split receiving the equity or non-equity consideration of the split enterprise.

Article 2 For the purpose of this Circular, equity consideration refers to a form of consideration paid by the acquiring enterprise in the form of an equity interest or shares of the acquiring enterprise or its controlled entity to purchase or exchange for the assets of another enterprise during the enterprise restructuring; and non-equity consideration refers to a form of payment by cash, bank deposits, receivables of the enterprise, and marketable securities other than its equity interests or shares or its controlled entity, including inventory, fixed assets, other assets and assumption of debt obligations.

Article 3 The tax treatment of enterprise restructuring shall be divided into general reorganisation and special reorganisation based on different criteria.

Article 4 The tax treatment with regards to enterprise restructuring, except where special reorganisation treatment is applicable as stipulated under this circular, shall generally be dealt with as follows:

  1. Where an enterprise turns from a legal person to a non-legal person organization such as a sole proprietorship or a partnership enterprise or has its registered place transferred to a place beyond the borders of the People's Republic of China (including the regions of Hong Kong, Macau and Taiwan), shall be deemed to be in liquidation, distributing its assets, and the shareholders making new investments and setting up a new enterprise. Tax base for all assets of the enterprise and shareholders' investment shall be determined on the basis of fair market value.

Where simple changes in other legal forms occurs in an enterprise, the tax registration may be directly altered; unless otherwise stipulated, payment of enterprise income tax (including the carry-over of losses, tax preference and other tax entitlement and tax obligations) shall be assumed by the enterprise that has been altered, unless there exist the ineligibility of tax preference as a result of change in the registered address.

  1. In debt restructuring, the relevant transactions shall be dealt with as follows:

(1) Where a debt obligation is settled with non-monetary assets, it should be divided into the transfer of non-monetary assets, and the settlement of debt obligation through non-monetary assets at fair value, with the gains or losses arising from the relevant assets recognised.

(2) Where a debt obligation is converted into equity,the transaction shall be divided into the liquidation of debts and equity investment, with the gains and losses from the repayment of debts recognised.

(3) The debtor shall recognise the gains from debt restructuring based on the amount by which the debt settlement falls short of the tax base of the debt obligation ; and the creditor shall determine the losses from the debt restructuring based on the amount by which the debt settlement amount received falls short of the tax base of the debt claim.

(4) The debtor's tax attributes shall, in principle, remain unchanged.

  1. In equity acquisition and asset acquisition of enterprise restructuring, the relevant transactions shall be dealt with as follows:

(1) The acquired party shall recognise the gains or losses from the transfer of equities and assets.

(2) The tax base of the equities or assets obtained by the acquiring party shall be determined on the basis of fair market value.

(3) The acquired enterprise's tax attributes shall, in principle, remain unchanged.

  1. In the merger of an enterprise, the parties involved shall be dealt with as follows:

(1) The merging enterprise shall determine the tax base for all assets and liabilities received from the merged enterprise on the basis of fair market value.

(2) The merged enterprise and its shareholders shall follow the enterprise income tax treatment as applicable to liquidation.

(3) The losses of the merged enterprise shall not be carried over to or be utilised by the merging enterprise.

  1. In the event of a split of enterprise, the parties involved shall be dealt with as follows:

(1) The enterprise being split shall recognise the gains or losses on the fair market value of the assets being split.

(2) The split enterprise shall determine the tax base of the split assets on the basis of fair market value.

(3) Where the enterprise being split continues to exist, the consideration obtained by its shareholders shall be deemed as distribution from the enterprise being split.

(4) Where the enterprise being split ceases to exist, the enterprise being split and its shareholders shall follow the enterprise income tax treatment of a liquidation.

(5) The losses of the enterprise being split shall not be carried over or utilised among the split enterprises.

Article 5 Where enterprise restructuring complies with all the following conditions , special reorganisation treatment shall apply:

  1. The enterprise restructuring has reasonable commercial purpose, and reduction, exemption or delay in tax payment is not its primary purposes.

  2. The ratio of acquired, merged or split assets or equities conforms to the provisions stipulated herein.

  3. The original substantive operating activities in respect of the restructured assets do not change within 12 consecutive months after the enterprise restructuring.

  4. The equity payment amount involved in the transaction consideration of the restructuring conforms with the ratio stipulated herein.

  5. The original major shareholders to whom the equities are paid in enterprise restructuring shall not transfer its equities obtained within 12 consecutive months after the restructuring.

Article 6 Where enterprise restructuring complies with the conditions provided in Article 5 hereof, the transaction parties may apply special tax treatment to the equity consideration in the transaction.

  1. Where the taxable incomes determined in the enterprise debt restructuring account for over 50% of the taxable income of such enterprise of the current year, it may be equally distributed to the taxable incomes of each year in the period of 5 years.

In the event there is a conversion of debt into equity, and the gains or losses of relevant debt repayment and equity investement are temporarily not determined, the tax base for equity investment shall be determined on the basis of the original tax base of the debt claim. Other income tax attributes shall remain unchanged.

  1. In the event of equity acquisition, where the equities purchased by the acquiring enterprise are not less than 75% of the total equities of the acquired enterprise, and the equity consideration amount of the acquiring enterprise incurred at the equity acquisition is not less than 85% of the total transaction payment, the following treatment may be selected:

(1) The tax base for the equities of the acquiring enterprise obtained by the shareholders of the acquired enterprise shall be determined based on the original tax base for the acquired equity interests.

(2) The tax base for the equities of the acquired enterprise obtained by the shareholders of the acquiring enterprise shall be determined based on the original tax base of the acquired equity interests.

(3) The tax base for all the original assets and liabilities of the acquiring and the acquired enterprises and other relevant issues on income tax attributes remain unchanged.

  1. In the event of asset acquisition, where the assets acquired by the receiving enterprise are not less than 75% of the total transferring enterprise's assets, and the equity consideration paid by the receiving enterprise is at least 85% of the total consideration, the following treatment may be selected:

(1) The tax base for the equities obtained by the transferring enterprise shall be determined based on the original tax base for the transferred assets.

(2) The tax base for the assets of the transferring enterprise obtained by the receiving enterprise shall be determined based on the original tax base for the transferred assets.

  1. In the event of a merger, where the merging enterprise's shareholders obtain an equity consideration of not less than 85% of of the amount of the consideration for the transaction, and in a merger where there is common equity control and there is no need to pay any consideration, the following treatment may be selected:

(1) The tax base for the assets and liabilities of the merged enterprises received by the merging enterprise shall be determined based on the original tax base of the merged enterprises.

(2) The income tax attributes of the merged enterprises prior to the merger shall be succeeded by the merging enterprise.

(3) The losses of the merged enterprises that could be utilized by the merging enterprise equals the fair market value of the net assets of the merged enterprises times the interest rate of the treasury bond with the longest maturity term issued by the State as of the end of the year in which the merger occurred.

(4) The tax base for the equities of the merging enterprise obtained by the shareholders of the merged enterprise shall be determined based on the tax base for equities of the merged enterprises originally held by the same.

  1. In the event of a split, where all the shareholders of the enterprise being split obtain equities of the split enterprise in the original shareholding ratio, both the split enterprise and the enterprise being split do not change their original substantive operating activities, and the shareholders of the enterprise being split obtain an equity consideration not less than 85% of their transaction consideration at the time of the split, the following treatment may be selected:

(1) The tax base for the assets and liabilities of the enterprise being split received by the split enterprise shall be determined based on the original tax base of the enterprise being split.

(2) The income tax attributes corresponding to the split assets of the enterprise being split shall be succeeded by the split enterprise.

(3) The losses of the enterprise being split that has not exceeded the statutory carry over period may be allocated in the proportion of the split assets to the total assets, and continue to be utilized by the split enterprise.

(4) In the event that the shareholders of enterprise being split need to surrender the equities originally held of the enterprise being split (hereinafter referred to as the "old equities") partially or fully when obtaining equities of the split enterprise (hereinafter referred to as the "new equities"), the tax base for the new equities shall be determined by the tax base for the old equities surrendered. In the event that the old equities need not be surrendered, then the tax base for the new equities obtained may be determined from one of the following two options: directly set the tax base for the new equities at zero; or by decreasing ratio of the split net assets of the enterprise being split to the total net assets of such enterprise, and then equally distribute the decreased tax base to the new equities.

  1. Where the parties involved in restructuring transactions do not determine the gains or losses of relevant assets temporarily in connection with equity consideration of the transaction in accordance with Item 1 to Item 5 of this article, the gains and losses from relevant assets transfer that correspond with the non-equity consideration shall still be determined, and the tax base for the corresponding assets shall be adjusted.

Gains or losses from asset transfer corresponding with non-equity consideration = (fair market value of the transferred assets - tax base for the transferred assets) x (non-equity consideration ÷ fair market value of the transferred assets)

Article 7 In the event that the enterprise engages in equity and asset acquisition transaction between China and an overseas territory(including Hong Kong, Macau and Taiwan regions), in addition to the conditions stipulated in Article 5 hereof, the following conditions should also be fulfilled in order to qualify for the special tax treatment provisions:

  1. When a non-resident enterprise transfers its equity interest in a resident enterprise to another non-resident enterprise in which it holds 100% direct interest, such transfer may not lead to a change in the capital gain withholding tax burden arising from the subsequent transfer of the enterprise shares, and the transferring non-resident enterprise undertakes to the competent taxation authority in writing that it shall not transfer the equities it possesses of the transferred non-resident enterprise within 3 years (including 3 years);

  2. A non-resident enterprise transfers the shares of a resident enterprise to its 100% directly owned resident enterprise;

  3. A resident enterprise invests the assets and equities in its possession in its 100% directly owned non-resident enterprise; and

  4. Other circumstances approved by the Ministry of Finance and the State Administration of Taxation.

Article 8 For the purpose of Item 3 of Article 7 hereof, where a resident enterprise invests the assets and equities in its possession in its 100% directly owned non-resident enterprise, and chooses to apply the special tax treatment, the gain from the transfer of the assets or equities may be equally included to the taxable incomes over a period of 10 fiscal years.

Article 9 In the event of merger by absorption, where the eligibility and the conditions for applicable tax preference of the surviving enterprise remain unchanged, the enterprise may continue to enjoy the tax incentive that has not expired prior to the merger, and the amount of tax incentive shall be calculated based on the taxable incomes (zero if taxable loss) of the previous year of the merger of the surviving enterprise.

In the event of a split, where the eligibility and the conditions for applicable tax incentive of the surviving enterprise remain unchanged, the enterprise may continue to enjoy the tax incentive of the that has not expired prior to the split, and the amount of incentive shall be calculated based on the taxable incomes (zero if taxable loss) of the previous year of the split of the enterprise multiplied by the ratio of the assets of the surviving enterprise after the separation to the total assets of such enterprise prior to the split.

Article 10 A series of assets and equities transaction taken progressively in the consecutive 12-month period prior to and after the restructuring, shall be treated as one enterprise restructuring transaction and dealt with as a whole based on the principle of substance over form.

Article 11 Where the enterprise complies with the special restructuring conditions stipulated herein and adopts special tax treatment, the parties involved shall submit written documents to the competent taxation authority to prove that it complies with the conditions stipulated for the special restructuring for the annual declaration of the enterprise income tax of the current year in which the restructuring occurs. Where the enterprise fails to file the documents in writing, special tax treatment shall not be applicable.

Article 12 Any other special enterprise income tax treatment with respect to enterprise restructuring will be separately addressed by the finance and taxation departments under the State Council.

Article 13 This Circular shall come into force as of January 1, 2008.
