Administrative Regulations of the People's Republic of China on the Registration of Enterprise by Legal Persons (Revised in 2014)

2014-02-19

Administrative Regulations of the People's Republic of China on the Registration of Enterprise by Legal Persons (Revised in 2014) (Edited by Lexiscn.com)

Order of the State Council No.648

February 19, 2014

(Promulgated under the Order of the State Council of the People's Republic of China No.1 on June 3, 1988, revised under Decision of the State Council on Repealing and Revising Certain Administrative Regulations promulgated on January 8, 2011 and revised for a second time under Decision of the State Council on Repealing and Revising Certain Administrative Regulations promulgated on February 19, 2014)

Chapter I General Provisions

Article 1 In accordance with the relevant provisions of the General Principles of Civil Law of the People's Republic of China, Administrative Regulations of the People's Republic of China on the Registration of Enterprise by Legal Persons (Revised in 2014) (hereinafter referred to as the "Regulations") are formulated with a view to establishing an administrative system for the registration of enterprises as legal persons, confirming the legal person status of enterprises, safeguarding the legitimate interests of enterprises, clamping down on illegal business operations, and maintaining the social and economic order.

Article 2 Any of the following enterprises satisfying legal person requirements shall go through registration in accordance with the relevant provisions of the Regulations:

  1. state-owned enterprises;

  2. enterprises under collective ownership;

  3. jointly-owned enterprises;

  4. Chinese-foreign equity joint ventures, Chinese-foreign cooperative joint ventures and wholly foreign-funded enterprises established within the territory of the People's Republic of China;

  5. privately-owned enterprises; and

  6. other enterprises required by the law to be registered as legal persons.

Article 3 Enterprises that apply to be registered as legal persons shall be given Business License for the Legal Entity and granted the status of legal persons when their applications for such registration have been examined and approved by the competent authorities in charge of the registration of enterprise by legal persons, and their legitimate rights and interests shall be protected by laws of the State.

Any enterprise which is required by law to be registered by legal person shall not engage in business activities without being approved and registered by the competent authorities in charge of the registration of enterprises by legal persons.

Chapter II Competent Registration Authorities

Article 4 The competent authorities in charge of the registration of enterprise by legal persons (hereinafter referred to as the "competent registration authorities") are the State Administration for Industry and Commerce and local administrative departments for industry and commerce at various levels. The competent registration authorities at various levels shall perform their functions in accordance with law under the leadership of the competent registration authorities at a higher level and be free from unlawful interference.

Article 5 The registration of national corporations, enterprise groups and corporations engaged in import-export business set up with the approval of the State Council or departments authorized by the State Council shall be examined and approved by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce. The registration of Chinese-foreign equity joint ventures, Chinese-foreign cooperative joint ventures and wholly foreign-funded enterprises shall be examined and approved by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce or by local administrative departments for industry and commerce authorized by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce.

The registration of subsidiary (or branch) companies of national corporations and enterprises, enterprise groups or companies engaged in import-export business established with the approval of the people's governments of provinces, autonomous regions or municipalities directly under the Central Government or departments authorized by them shall be examined and approved by the administrative departments for industry and commerce of the provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the Central Government.

The registration of other enterprises shall be examined and approved by the administrative departments for industry and commerce of cities or counties (districts) in which the enterprises are located.

Article 6 The competent registration authorities at various levels shall establish a record for the registration of enterprise by legal persons and a statistical system for such registration and collect basic information about the registration of enterprise by legal persons so as to serve the development of a planned commodity economy.

The competent registration authorities shall, in a planned manner and according to the needs of society, make available to the general public data about the registration of enterprise by legal persons.

Chapter III Conditions for Registration and Entities to Apply for Registration

Article 7 Any entity that applies to be registered as enterprise by legal persons must satisfy the following requirements:

  1. the entity has name, organizational structure and articles of association;

  2. the entity has a fixed place of business and essential facilities;

  3. the entity has funds and employees in conformity with State regulations and in line with its scale of production, operation or service;

  4. the entity is able to bear civil liabilities independently; and

  5. the entity has a scope of business in conformity with the provisions of the relevant laws, regulations and policies of the State.

Article 8 The application of an enterprise for registration with legal person status shall be filed by the person responsible for establishing the enterprise.

The registration with legal person status of a jointly-owned enterprise which bears civil liabilities independently shall be applied for by the person responsible for establishing the enterprise.

Chapter IV Registration Matters

Article 9 Major matters involved in the registration of an enterprise with legal person are as follows:

Its name, domicile, place of business, legal representative, economic nature, scope of business, mode of operation, registered capital, number of employees, duration of operation, and branches and subsidiaries.

Article 10 An enterprise with legal person status shall use only one name. The name to be used by the enterprise with legal person status in its application for registration shall be examined by the competent registration authorities and, after it is approved and registered, the enterprise with legal person status shall be entitled to the exclusive use of the registered name within a specified scope.

Those who apply to establish Chinese-foreign equity joint ventures, Chinese-foreign cooperative joint ventures or wholly foreign-funded enterprises shall apply to the competent registration authorities for registering the names of the enterprises before their contracts and articles of association are examined and approved.

Article 11 The legal representative of an enterprise with legal person status, which has been registered after examination and approval by the competent registration authority, shall be the signatory who exercises functions and powers on behalf of the enterprise. The signature of the legal representative shall be submitted to the competent registration authority for the record.

Article 12 The registered capital of an enterprise with legal person status represents the total value of the property the State entrusts to it for operation and management or that of the property owned by the enterprise itself.

When an enterprise with legal person status makes a registration for the commencement of business, the discrepancy between the sum of capital it applies for registration and the sum of capital it actually possesses shall be handled in accordance with specific regulations of the State.

Article 13 The scope of business of an enterprise with legal person status shall be consistent with its funds, sites, equipment, employees and technical force. It may focus on one line of business as its main operation while engaging in other operations simultaneously in accordance with relevant regulations of the State. An enterprise with legal person status shall engage in business activities within such a scope of business as is approved and registered.

Chapter V Registration for Commencement of Business

Article 14 An enterprise with legal person status shall apply to the competent registration authority to make a registration for the commencement of business within 30 days after the approval of the competent department of it or that of an authority for examination and approval. For an enterprise for which there is no department in charge or authority for examination and approval, its application for registration for the commencement of business shall be examined by the competent registration authority. The competent registration authority shall make a decision on whether or not to approve the application for registration within 30 days after receiving it.

Article 15 An enterprise with legal person status shall submit the following papers and documents when applying for making a registration for the commencement of business:

  1. an application for registration signed by the person responsible for its establishment;

  2. the document of approval issued by the competent department or the authority for examination and approval;

  3. its articles of association;

  4. a certificate of its creditworthiness, a certificate of the verification of its capital or a guarantee for its capital;

  5. a certificate of the identity of the principal responsible person of the enterprise;

  6. a certificate of the right to use its domicile and place of business; and

  7. other relevant papers and documents.

Article 16 Where an entity files an application for the commencement of business as an enterprise with legal person status, upon being approved and registered by the competent registration authority and granted a Business Licenses for Enterprise by Legal Persons, the enterprise is established. The enterprise by legal persons may henceforth have its official seal made, open a bank account, sign contracts and conduct business operations on the basis of its Business License for the Legal Entity.

The competent registration authority may, after a check-up, issue a counterpart of the Business License for the Legal Entity, if the enterprise with legal person status needs them for its business operations.

Chapter VI Changes in Registration

Article 17 An enterprise with legal person status shall apply for making changes in its registration whenever it changes its name, domicile, place of business, legal representative, economic nature, scope of business, mode of operation, registered capital or duration of operation or whenever it increases or reduces its branches.

Article 18 An enterprise with legal person status shall apply to the competent registration authority for making changes in its registration within 30 days after the changes are approved by the competent department or an examination and approval authority.

Article 19 An enterprise with legal person status shall apply to the competent registration authority for making changes in its registration, or for making a registration for the commencement of business or for canceling its registration, whenever it is split up, or merged with others or moved elsewhere, within 30 days after these changes are approved by the competent department or an examination and approval authority.

Chapter VII Cancellation of Registration

Article 20 An enterprise with legal person status shall go through the procedures for canceling its registration with the competent registration authority when it winds up, is removed from the register of enterprises, is declared bankrupt or terminates its business operations for other reasons.

Article 21 Whenever an enterprise with legal person status is to cancel its registration, it must submit an application signed by its legal representative, a document of approval issued by the competent department or an examination and approval authority, a certificate showing the completion of the settlement of its liabilities, or a document showing that a liquidation organization will be responsible for settling its claims and liabilities. The competent registration authority, after examining and approving the application, shall take back the Business License for the Legal Entity, the counterpart of the License and the official seal of the enterprise, and notify banks at which it has opened an account of the cancellation of its registration.

Article 22 An enterprise with legal person status, which fails to start business six months after receiving its Business License for the Legal Entity or which has ceased its operations for a year, shall be regarded as having closed down, and the registration authority take back the Business License for the Legal Entity, the counterpart of the License and the official seal of the enterprise, and notify banks at which it has opened an account of the cancellation of its registration.

Chapter VIII Publicity and Certificates and Licenses Administration

Article 23 The competent registration authorities shall publicize the information on registration and filing of enterprise with legal person status to the society through the enterprise credit information publicity system.

Article 24 An enterprise legal person shall submit the annual report for the previous year to the competent registration authorities and publicize the same to the society through the enterprise credit information publicity system from January 1 through June 30 each year.

The contents publicized in the annual report and the supervision and inspection thereof shall be subject to the provisions of the State Council.

Article 25 The Business License for the Legal Entity issued by the competent registration authority is the certificate of an enterprise with legal person status. Other than the competent registration authority, which may impound or revoke such certificate in accordance with legal procedures, no organ or individual shall take over, detain or destroy such certificate.

An enterprise with legal person status, which has lost its Business License for the Legal Entity or the counterpart of the License, must announce the loss in a newspaper before it can apply for a replacement.

The Business License for the Legal Entity and its counterpart may not be forged, altered, leased, lent, transferred or sold.

The State carries out electronic business licenses. An electronic business license has the same legal force and effect as a paper business license.

Article 26 An enterprise with legal person status shall pay registration fees in accordance with relevant provisions when it makes a registration for the commencement of business, or changes in its registration. The fee to be charged for a registration for the commencement of business shall be 0.1% of the sum of the enterprise's registered capital; in case that the registered capital exceeds CNY10 million, the fee to be charged on any portion in excess of the aforesaid sum shall be 0.05% of it; in case that the registered capital exceeds CNY100 million, no fees shall be charged on any portion in excess of the aforesaid sum. The minimum registration fee shall be CNY50. Fees to be charged for making changes in the registration shall be prescribed by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce.

Chapter IX Administration of the Registration of Business Operations by Public institutions and Scientific and Technological Social Groups

Article 27 When public institutions or scientific and technological social groups establish enterprises qualified as legal persons in line with relevant regulations of the state, the applications for registration shall be filed by the enterprises. They may engage in business operations only after their applications for registration have been approved by the competent registration authorities and after they have received their respective Business License for the Legal Entity.

Article 28 Public institutions, which are run like enterprises in accordance with relevant regulations of the State and which no longer receive operating funds from the State, or scientific and technological social groups which are established for business operations, shall apply for registration if they are qualified as enterprise by legal persons. They may engage in business operations only after their applications have been approved by the registration authorities and they have received their respective Business License for the Legal Entity.

Chapter X Supervision and Administration

Article 29 The competent registration authorities shall exercise the following functions of supervision and administration over enterprise by legal persons in accordance with the law:

  1. supervising enterprise by legal persons in making a registration for the commencement of business or changes, or canceling its registration in accordance with relevant provisions;

  2. supervising enterprises with legal person in carrying out business activities in line with the matters of registration, the articles of association and any contracts;

  3. supervising the compliance of enterprise by legal persons and their legal representatives with laws, regulations and policies of the State; and

  4. controlling, investigating or dealing with illegal business activities of enterprises legal person status and protecting their legitimate rights and interests.

Article 30 The registration authority may, in light of the circumstances involved, penalize an enterprise with legal person status by warning, fine, confiscation of illegal earnings, suspension of business for remedial action, or revoking the Business License for the Legal Entity, if it falls under any of the following circumstances:

  1. where actual facts are concealed or there is fraud in its registration or the enterprise commences business activities without being approved and making a registration;

  2. where major matters involved in its registration is changed without permission or the enterprise carries out business activities beyond such a scope of business as is approved and registered;

  3. where the enterprise fails to cancel its registration in accordance with relevant provisions;

  4. where the enterprise forges, alters, leases, lends, transfers or sells without permission the Business License for the Legal Entity or any counterpart of such License ;

  5. where the enterprise withdraws or transfers capital or conceal property in order to dodge any liabilities; or

  6. where the enterprise carries out any illegal business activities.

When penalizing an enterprise with legal person status in accordance with the above provisions, the competent registration authority shall pursue its legal representative for administrative and economic liability according to circumstances involved in any violations of the law; where the criminal law is violated, judicial organs shall pursue the legal representative for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Article 31 The competent registration authority shall ascertain the facts and act in accordance with law when dealing with the illegal activities of an enterprise with legal person status and notify the parties concerned of its decision in writing.

Article 32 When an enterprise with legal person status disagrees with any penalty given by the competent registration authority, it may appeal against any such penalty within 15 days after receiving the notice of penalty, to the competent registration at the next higher level for review. The higher registration authority shall make a review decision within 30 days after receiving the appeal for review. The enterprise may file an action in a people's court within 30 days after receiving the notice of review if it disagrees with the review decision. The competent registration authority may, in accordance with the prescribed procedures, apply to the people's court for compulsory execution, if the enterprise fails to appeal or to pay the fine or the confiscated sum at the expiry of the prescribed period.

Article 33 When an enterprise with legal person status has its business license for the legal entity revoked, the competent registration authority shall take over its official seal and notify the bank at which it has an account of the cancellation of its registration, and the competent department or a liquidation organization shall be responsible for settling its claims and liabilities.

Article 34 Any staff member of the competent department, an examination and approval authority or the competent registration authority who has violated the Regulations, neglected his duties severely, abused his powers, practiced graft, extorted and taken bribes or encroached on the legitimate rights and interests of an enterprise with legal person status, shall be given an administrative or economic penalty depending on the circumstances involved; where criminal laws are violated, a judicial organ pursue any such staff member for criminal liability in accordance with the law.

Chapter XI Supplementary Provisions

Article 35 When an enterprise with legal person status establishes a branch which is incapable of bearing civil liability independently, the registration of the branch shall be applied for by the enterprise. The branch shall receive a Business License after the application is approved by the registration authority and may engage in business operations within the scope of business as approved in registration.

In accordance with relevant State regulations, administrative public institutions depending on State funding or scientific and technological social bodies must apply for registration if they engage in business operations or establish enterprises not qualified as legal persons. They shall receive Business Licenses after their applications are approved by the competent registration authorities and may engage in business operations within the scope of business as approved in registration.

With respect to the specific control of the registration involved, the Regulations shall apply mutatis mutandis.

Article 36 For new enterprises to be established with the approval of relevant departments of the State Council or planning departments at various levels, if their preparations have been under way for more than one year, applications for the registration of the establishment shall be filed in accordance special provisions.

Article 37 Where, before the Regulations take effect, any enterprise qualified as a legal person has been approved by and made a registration with the competent registration authority, any such enterprise is not required to make a registration for enterprise by legal persons.

Article 38 The State Administration for Industry and Commerce shall be responsible for interpreting the Regulations; and the rules for their implementation shall also be formulated by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce.

Article 39 The Regulations shall come into force as of July 1, 1988. The Regulations for Controlling the Registration of Chinese-Foreign Equity Joint Ventures promulgated by the State Council on July 26, 1980, the Regulations for Controlling the Registration of Industrial and Commercial Enterprises promulgated by the State Council on August 9, 1982, and the Interim Provisions for Controlling the Registration of Companies approved by the State Council on August 14, 1985 and promulgated by the State Administration for Industry and Commerce on August 25, 1985 shall be abolished on the same date.
