Decision of the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce on Amending the Provisions on Merging and Splitting of Foreign-Funded Enterprises

2001/11/22

Decision of the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce on Amending the Provisions on Merging and Splitting of Foreign-Funded Enterprises

Order of the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce [2001] No. 8

November 22, 2001

The Decision of the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce on Amending the Provisions on Merging and Splitting of Foreign-Funded Enterprises is hereby released and shall come into effect as of the day of the promulgation.

Minister: Shi Guangsheng

Director: Wang Zhongfu

Attachment: Decision of the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce on Amending the Provisions on Merging and Splitting of Foreign-Funded Enterprises

To meet the new requirements of reform and opening up, further improve the laws and regulations for foreign investment in China, and to standardize the merger of foreign-funded enterprises and Chinese domestic-funded enterprises, the Provisions on the Merging and Splitting of Foreign-Funded Enterprises (Wai Jing Mao Fa Fa [1999] No. 395) is amended as follows in accordance with the Company Law of the People's Republic of China and other laws and administrative provisions on foreign-funded enterprises:

Article 1 Article 9 is revised as Companies shall not be merged or split before they pay their capital, provide conditions for cooperation, and actually start production and operation in accordance with the companies contract and the articles of association. Where the investors have already paid the capital, provided the conditions for cooperation, the companies may merge with Chinese domestic-funded enterprises.

Article 2 Add after Article 17: Article 18 Where the companies and Chinese domestic-funded enterprises merge into a foreign-funded enterprise, the total amount of investment thereof shall be the summation of the total amount of investment of the original companies and the total amount of enterprise assets recorded in the financial auditing reports of the Chinese domestic-funded enterprises. The registered capital shall be the summation of the registered capital of the original company and that of the Chinese domestic-funded enterprise The proportion that the registered capital in the total amount of investment of the merged company shall be in accordance with the Tentative Provisions on the Proportion of Registered Capital in the Total Amount of Investment of Chinese-foreign Equity Joint Ventures of the SAIC; where those Provisions can be carried out under special circumstances, approval from the MOFTEC together with the SAIC is required.

And Article 19 Where the enterprises already established by the Chinese domestic-funded enterprises merging with the companies become the enterprises in which the companies hold shares, the requirements of China industrial policies on the use of foreign investment and the Tentative Provisions on Investment Made by Foreign-funded Enterprises within China shall be complied with. The merged company shall not hold stock equities in the enterprises of the industries in which foreign investment is prohibited.

Article 3 Add in the original Article 18: Where the companies merge with Chinese domestic-funded enterprises, the applicant shall also submit the copies of the business licenses of the enterprises already established by those Chinese domestic-funded enterprises to be merged to the authorities for examination and approval.

Article 4 Add in the original Article 35: Where the companies merge with Chinese domestic-funded enterprises, the companies continuing to exist or newly established shall also go through the relevant procedures for examination and verification with the departments of taxation, the customs, land administration and foreign exchange control in accordance with the relevant provisions on the foreign-funded enterprises.

Article 5 Add a second paragraph in the original Article 36: Where the foreign investors purchase the stock equities of the shareholders of the Chinese domestic-funded enterprises in the process of the merging the companies with Chinese domestic-funded enterprises, the conditions for payment for the stock equities shall be in accordance with the Supplementary Provisions to Several Provisions Concerning the Investment Made by the Various Parties to Chinese-foreign Equity Joint Ventures .

Article 6 The original Article 39 is revised as: These Provisions shall come into effect as of the date of promulgation.

In addition, the statement of some clauses shall be amended correspondingly, and the order of the articles, clauses and items shall be adjusted correspondingly.

This Decision shall come into effect as of the date of promulgation.

Provisions on the Merging and Splitting of Foreign-Funded Enterprises shall be amended in accordance with this Decision and be promulgated again.

Attachment: Decision of the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce on Amending the Provisions on Merging and Splitting of Foreign-Funded Enterprises

Promulgated by the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation through No. 395 [1999] on September 23, 1999;

Amended in accordance with the Decision of the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce on Amending the Provisions on Merger and Split-up of Foreign-Funded Enterprises issued on November 22, 2001.

Article 1 To standardize the actions related to merger and split-up of foreign-funded enterprises and to protect the legal rights and interests of investors of enterprises and creditors, these Provisions are enacted in accordance with the Company Law of the People' Republic of China and with laws and administrative regulations on foreign-funded enterprises.

Article 2 These Provisions are applicable to the merging between or the splitting of Chinese-foreign equity joint ventures, Chinese-foreign contractual joint ventures with legal person status, wholly foreign-funded enterprises, and foreign-funded joint-stock limited companies (hereinafter universally referred to as companies) that are established within the Chinese territory in accordance with the Chinese laws.

Merging between the companies and Chinese domestic-funded enterprises shall be handled in reference to the relevant laws, regulations and these Provisions.

Article 3 Merging as used in these Provisions refers to the merging into one company of two or more companies through signing an agreement in accordance with the rules of the Company Law.

Merging of companies may take the forms of merger and consolidation.

Merger denotes that a company absorbs another or others and remains in existence while the absorbed is dissolved.

Consolidation denotes that two or more companies merge into one new company and the original ones are all dissolved.

Article 4 Splitting as used in these Provisions refers to the fact that one company is divided into two or more companies through decisions made by the supreme power body of the company in accordance with the Company Law.

Splitting may take the forms of split-off and split-up.

Split-off refers to the fact that one company is divided into two or more companies while the company remains to exist after the establishment of one or more new companies.

Split-up refers to the fact that one company is divided into two or more companies while the company itself is dissolved after the establishment of two or more new companies.

Article 5 Merging or splitting of companies shall abide by the Chinese laws, regulations and these Provisions, shall follow the principle of self-willingness, equality and fair competition, and shall not be harmful to the social public interests or to the legal rights and interests of the creditors.

Merging or splitting of companies shall be accorded with the provisions of the Tentative Provisions on Guiding the Orientation of Foreign Investment and of the Catalogue for the Encouragement of Foreign Investment Industries, and shall not lead to solo investment, share-controlling or dominance status by foreign investors in companies of industries where solo investment, share-controlling or dominance status by foreign investors are not allowed.

Alteration in industry or scope of business of companies as a result of merging or splitting shall comply with the relevant laws, regulations and provisions of the state industrial policies, and shall go through the approval procedures.

Article 6 Merging or splitting of companies shall be accorded with the provisions issued by the departments of the Customs, taxation and foreign exchange. After verified by the departments of examination and approval, the Customs and taxation, etc., companies which continue to exist or are newly established after merging or splitting shall continue to enjoy the same various treatments of foreign-funded enterprises as they enjoyed originally.

Article 7 Merging or splitting of companies shall be examined and approved by the original departments for examination and approval, and shall go to the registration department for the registration of establishment, alteration or cancellation.

Where there exist more than two original departments for examination and approval for the companies to be merged, the registration departments authorized by the department of foreign trade and economic cooperation in the place of residence of the merged companies and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce (hereinafter referred to as SAIC for short) shall be the institutions functioning for examination and approval and registration.

Where the total amount of investment of the companies to be merged exceeds the power of the original authorities for examination and approval or that of the authorities for examination and approval in the place of residence of the merged companies, the merging shall be examined and approved by the competent authorities with the power for examination and approval.

Where at least one of the companies to be merged is joint-stock limited company, the merging shall be examined and approved by the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation (hereinafter referred to as MOFTEC for short).

Article 8 For dissolution of the original companies or establishment of new companies in different places owing to merging or splitting, opinions should be sought from the authorities for examination and approval in the place where the companies to be dissolved or established are located.

Article 9 Companies shall not be merged or split before they pay their capital, provide conditions for cooperation and actually start production and operation in accordance with the companies contract and the articles of association. Where the investors have already paid the capital, provided the conditions for cooperation, the companies may merge with Chinese domestic-funded enterprises.

Article 10 Merging between liability limited companies will result in a liability limited company. Merging between joint-stock limited companies will result in a joint-stock limited company.

Merging between listed joint-stock limited companies and liability limited companies will result in a joint-stock limited company. Merging between non-listed joint-stock limited companies and liability limited companies will result in either a joint-stock limited company or a liability limited company after.

Article 11 In case of merging between the joint-stock limited companies or where the companies merge into a limited liability company, the registered capital of the merged company shall be the total amount of registered capital of the original companies.

Where the liability limited companies and joint-stock limited companies merge into one joint-stock limited company, the registered capital of the merged company shall be the totality of the value of shares converted into from the value of net assets each share of the original liability limited companies contains and the total shares of the original joint-stock limited companies.

Article 12 In case of merging between companies accorded with Clause one of Article 11 of these Provisions, the stock equity proportion of the investing parties to the merged company shall be determined, as stipulated by the state, through negotiations between the investors or in accordance with the appraisal result of the stock equity value of the original company done by an assets evaluation institution, and shall be determined in the contract and the articles of association of the merged company. But the equity proportion of the foreign party shall not be below 25% of the registered capital of the merged company.

Article 13 The amount of registered capital of each of the split companies shall be determined by the body of supreme power of the original company, in accordance with the relevant laws and regulations concerning foreign-funded enterprises and regulations of the registration institutions. But the total amount of registered capital of each of the split companies shall be equal to the total amount of registered capital of the original company.

Article 14 The stock equity proportion of all investors in each of the split companies shall be determined by the investors in the contracts and the articles of association of the split companies, but the equity proportion of a foreign party shall not be below 25% of the registered capital of the split company.

Article 15 Where the merging of companies takes the form of merger, the date of establishment of the absorbing company shall be the date of establishment of the merged company; for that taking the form of consolidation, the date on which the registration institution gives its approval for the establishment registration and issues the business license shall be the date of the establishment of the merged company.

Where any new company is established as a result of splitting, the date on which the registration institution gives its approval for the establishment registration and issues the business license shall be the date of the establishment of the split company.

Article 16 Where the merging or splitting involves any listed joint-stock limited company, it must be acted in conformity with the relevant laws and regulations and the regulations on listed companies issued by the departments for supervision of securities under the State Council, and shall go through necessary procedures for examination and approval.

Article 17 Merging between the companies and Chinese domestic-funded enterprises shall be carried out in accordance with the Chinese laws and regulations on the use of foreign investment and China's industrial policies, and shall meet the following conditions:

  1. The Chinese domestic-funded enterprises to be merged must be liability limited companies or joint-stock limited companies established in accordance with the norms stipulated in the Company Law of the People's Republic of China;

  2. The investors must meet the qualifications required for the investors engaged in the related industries of the merged company as stipulated in the relevant laws, regulations and rules by the departments concerned;

  3. The stock equity proportion of the foreign party shall not be below 25% of the registered capital of the merged company;

  4. All parties to the agreement on merging shall guarantee full employment of their original staff members and workers of the companies to be merged or make rational arrangement.

Article 18 Where the companies and Chinese domestic-funded enterprises merge into a foreign-funded enterprise, the total amount of investment thereof shall be the summation of the total amount of investment of the original companies and the total amount of enterprise assets recorded in the financial auditing reports of the Chinese domestic-funded enterprises. The registered capital is the summation shall be the registered capital of the original company and that of the Chinese domestic-funded enterprise. The proportion that the registered capital takes in the total amount of investment of the merged company shall be in accordance with the Tentative regulations on the Proportion of Registered Capital in the Total Amount of Investment of Chinese-foreign Equity Joint Ventures of the SAIC; where those Provisions can's be carried out under special circumstances, approval from the MOFTEC together with the SAIC is required.

Article 19 Where the enterprises already established by the Chinese domestic-funded enterprises merging with the companies become the enterprises in which the companies hold shares, the requirements of China industrial policies on the use of foreign investment and the Tentative Provisions on Investment Made by Foreign-funded Enterprises within China shall be complied with. The merged company shall not hold stock equities in the enterprises of the industries in which foreign investment is prohibited.

Article 20 In case of merger of companies, the absorbing party should be the applicant. In case of consolidation of companies, an applicant should be determined through negotiations of all parties.

The following documents should be submitted by the applicant to the authorities for examination and approval:

  1. Application for the merging of the companies and agreement on merging of the companies signed by the legal representatives of all companies concerned;

  2. Decision on the merging of companies made by the supreme authorities of each of the companies;

  3. Contracts and the articles of association of all companies;

  4. Copies of approval certificates and business licenses of all companies;

  5. Credit examination reports of all companies provided by authorized Chinese institutions for credit examination;

  6. Balance sheets and property lists of all companies;

  7. Audit reports of the previous year of all companies;

  8. Lists of creditors of all companies;

  9. Contracts and the articles of association of the merged company;

  10. Membership list of the body with supreme power of the merged company;

  11. Other documents required by the authorities for examination and approval.

Where the companies merge with Chinese domestic-funded enterprises, the applicant shall also submit the copies of the business licenses of the enterprises already established by those Chinese domestic-funded enterprises to be merged to the authorities for examination and approval.

Article 21 The agreement on merging of companies should include the following contents:

  1. Names, addresses and legal representatives of all parties to the agreement on merging;

  2. Name, address and legal representative of the merged company;

  3. The total amount of investment and registered capital of the merged company;

  4. The form of merging;

  5. The succession plan for creditors' rights and debts of all parties to the agreement on merging;

  6. Measures for the arrangement of the staff members and workers;

  7. Responsibilities for breach of contract;

  8. Ways for settlement of disputes;

  9. Date and place for signing agreement;

  10. Other items deemed necessary to stipulate by the contractual parties to the agreement on merging.

Article 22 Where companies to be merged involve two or more original authorities for examination and approval, the companies to be dissolved should submit an application for dissolution to the original authorities for examination and approval before they submit the relevant documents in accordance with Article 18 of these Provisions to the authorities for examination and approval.

The original authorities for examination and approval should give a reply within 15 days upon the date of the receipt of the application for dissolution as stipulated in the preceding Clause. Where the original authorities for examination and approval fail to give a reply within 15 days, it will be deemed that the original authorities for examination and approval have agreed to the dissolution of the company.

When the original authorities for examination and approval give a negative reply within the period as stipulated in the preceding Clause to the company's application for dissolution, the company to be dissolved may submit the application for dissolution to the responsible department of foreign trade and economic cooperation at a higher level to both the original authorities for examination and approval and the authorities for examination and approval of the merging, and the said department shall give a judgment within 30 days upon the receipt of the application for dissolution of the company.

If the authorities for examination and approval disagree or do not approve the merging of companies, the reply on the dissolution of the companies shall become invalid automatically.

Article 23 A company to be split should submit to the authorities for examination and approval the following documents:

  1. Application for splitting of the company signed by the legal representative of the company;

  2. Decision on splitting of the company by the body of supreme power of the company;

  3. Agreement on splitting of the company signed by companies that remain to exist or to be newly established (hereinafter referred to as parties to the agreement on splitting) owing to the splitting;

  4. Contract and the articles of association of the company;

  5. Copies of approved certificates and the business license of the company;

  6. Credit examination reports of the company provided by authorized Chinese institutions for credit examination;

  7. Balance sheet and property list of the company;

  8. List of creditors of the company;

  9. Contracts and articles of association of all split companies;

  10. Membership lists of the bodies with supreme power of the split companies;

  11. Other documents required by the authorities for examination and approval.

Where any company is newly established in different places, the company shall also submit the opinions on the establishment of the new company signed by the authorities for examination and approval in the place where the company to be established is located.

Article 24 The agreement on splitting of a company should include the following contents:

  1. Names, addresses and legal representatives of the parties to the agreement on splitting;

  2. The total amount of investment and registered capital of the split companies;

  3. The form of splitting;

  4. Plan agreed on by the parties to the agreement on splitting for property partition of the company to be split;

  5. The succession plan agreed on by the parties to the agreement on splitting for creditors' rights and debts of the company to be split;

  6. Measures for the arrangement of the employees;

  7. Responsibilities for breach of contract;

  8. Ways for the settlement of disputes;

  9. Date and place for signing agreement;

  10. Other items deemed necessary by the parties to the agreement on splitting.

Article 25 Companies continuing to exist or companies to be newly established after merging shall have to succeed all of the creditors rights and debts of the companies dissolved as a result of the merging.

The split companies shall succeed the creditors rights and debts of the original company in accordance with the agreement on splitting.

Article 26 The authorities for examination and approval should give an initial reply in written form on whether they agree to the merging or splitting within 45 days upon the receipt of the submitted documents as prescribed in Article 18 or 21 of these Provisions.

In case the merging with other companies are to be examined and approved by the MOFTEC, and that the MOFTEC considers that the merging will have a trend of industry monopoly or will possibly form a market-controlling status of some of the specific commodities or services that may hinder fair competition, it may call, after the receipt of the documents prescribed in the previous Clause, a hearing of the companies to be merged, which is attended by the departments and institutions concerned, and may organize an investigation on the companies and the relevant market. In such situation, the period for examination and approval as prescribed in the preceding Clause may be extended to 180 days.

Article 27 Companies to be merged or split should send out a notification to the creditors within 10 days upon the date on which the authorities for examination and approval give an initial written reply, agreeing to the merging or splitting of companies, and should publish it at least for 3 times within 30 days in the newspapers distributed nationwide at or above the provincial level.

The companies should explain in the aforesaid notification and announcement the plan for debt succession of the existing companies.

Article 28 Creditors of the companies have the right to ask the companies to revise their plan for debts succession within 30 days from the date of the receipt of the notification prescribed in Article 25 of these Provisions, or to ask the companies to clear off debts or provide guarantee for the debts. Creditors not receiving the notification enjoy the same right within 90 days from the date on which the announcement is made.

In case creditors of the companies did not exercise the right within the period prescribed in the preceding Clause, it would be deemed that the creditors had agreed to the succession plan for creditor's rights and debts of the companies to be merged or split, and that the creditors' claim shall have no effect on the merging or splitting of the companies.

Article 29 If creditors of the companies to be merged or split have no objection within 90 days from the date on which the first announcement of merging or splitting is made, the applicant of companies to be merged or the companies to be split should submit to the authorities for examination and approval the following documents:

  1. Proof for the announcement published by the companies for three times in the newspapers about their merging or splitting;

  2. Proof for the notification given by the companies to their creditors;

  3. Statement made by the companies about the handling of creditors' rights and debts;

  4. Other documents deemed necessary by the authorities for examination and approval.

Article 30 The authorities for examination and approval should decide within 30 days from the date of the receipt of the documents prescribed in Article 27 of these Provisions on whether or not they will approve the merging or splitting of companies.

Article 31 Where the companies take the form of merger, the absorbing company should go to the original authorities for examination and approval for alteration of the approval certificate for foreign-funded enterprise, and register with the registration department for the change; the absorbed companies should go to the original authorities for examination and approval for turning in the approval certificates for foreign-funded enterprise, and to the registration department for canceling the registration of the companies.

Where the companies take the form of consolidation, all the parties of the companies merged should go to the original authorities for examination and approval for turning in their approval certificates for foreign-funded enterprise, and to the registration department for canceling registration of the companies; the newly established companies should go, through its applicant, to the authorities for examination and approval for drawing the approval certificates for foreign-funded enterprise, and to the registration department for registration of the establishment of the companies.

Where the companies take the form of split-off, the company that continues to exist should go to the authorities for examination and approval for alteration of the approval certificate for foreign-funded enterprise, and register with the registration department for the alteration; the newly established companies should go to the authorities for examination and approval for drawing the approval certificates for foreign-funded enterprises, and to the registration department for registration of the establishment of the companies

Where the companies adopt the form of split-up, the original company should go to the original authorities for examination and approval for turning in its approval certificate for foreign-funded enterprise, and to the registration department for canceling registration of the company; the newly established companies should go to the authorities for examination and approval for drawing the approval certificates for foreign-funded enterprise, and to the registration department for registration of the establishment of the companies.

Where the companies merge with Chinese domestic-funded enterprises, only the companies need to go through the relevant procedures for the approval certificates for foreign-funded enterprises.

Article 32 Applicants for companies merged or companies to be split should, within 30 days from the date on which the authorities for examination and approval give approval for the merging or splitting, go to the correspondent authorities for examination and approval for matters of dissolution, continuous existence or establishment of new companies as a result of the merging or splitting, and go through procedures for turning in, altering or drawing the approval certificates for foreign-funded enterprise.

Article 33 The companies should go to the registration department from the date of turning in, altering or drawing the approval certificates for foreign-funded enterprise, and go through the procedures for canceling the registration or for the registration of alteration or establishment in accordance with the relevant provisions ofthe Regulations of the People's Republic of China for Controlling the Registration of Enterprises as Legal Person and the Administrative Regulations of the People's Republic of China on Company Registration

Registration for establishment should be made after the registration of alteration or cancellation of registration of the relevant company has been finished.

The disposition plan of company property and the succession plan for creditors rights and debts stated in the agreement on the merging or splitting of companies, and the documents of the authorities for examination and approval for approving the companies merging or splitting shall be regarded as the liquidation reports that need to be presented for registration cancellation.

Article 34 After the companies go through the procedures for cancellation of registration and for registration of alteration for the merger or split-up, the parties shall bear corresponding legal liabilities if they fail to go through, in accordance with the laws, the relevant procedures for the registration of establishment of the companies

Article 35 The revised contracts and the articles of association signed by the investors of the companies as a result of the merging or splitting of companies shall become effective on the date on which the authorities for examination and approval give approval for alteration or issue after verifying the approval certificates for foreign-funded enterprises.

Article 36 Companies merged or continuing to exist or newly established should give a notification of the alteration of creditors and debtors, within 30 days from the date of making alteration or receiving the business licenses, to the creditors and debtors of the companies dissolved as a result of the merging or splitting, and should make an announcement in the newspapers distributed nationwide at or above the provincial level.

Article 37 Companies merged or continuing to exist or newly established should go through registration procedures, within 30 days from the date of changing or receiving the business licenses, with the departments of taxation, the Customs, land administration and foreign exchange control.

Where the companies merge with Chinese domestic-funded enterprises, the companies continuing to exist or newly established shall also go through the relevant procedures for examination and verification with the departments of taxation, the Customs, land administration and foreign exchange control in accordance with the relevant provisions on the foreign-funded enterprises.

Article 38 Where any stock equity transfer is involved in the process of company merging or splitting, it shall be handled in accordance with the laws, regulations and rules concerning the change of investors stock equities of foreign-funded enterprises.

Where the foreign investors purchase the stock equities of the shareholders of the domestic-funded enterprises in the process of the merging with the companies with Chinese domestic-funded enterprises, the conditions for payment for the stock equities shall be in accordance with the Supplementary Provisions of the Several Provisions on the Capital Contribution of the Parties Concerned to Chinese-Foreign Equity Joint Ventures.

Article 39 The merging or splitting of companies in Chinese mainland by investors from Hong Kong, Macao and Taiwan Province shall be handled in accordance with these Provisions.

Article 40 The power to interpret these Provisions shall remain with the Ministry of Foreign Trade and Economic Cooperation and the State Administration for Industry and Commerce.

Article 41 These Provisions shall come into effect as of the date of promulgation.
