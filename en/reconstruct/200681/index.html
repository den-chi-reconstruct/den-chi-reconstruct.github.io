Circular of SAIC, MOFCOM, GAC and SAFE on Printing and Issuing Suggestions on Implementation of Several Issues Applicable for Law of Examination, Approval, Registration and Administration of Foreign-funded Enterprises

2006/04/24

Circular of SAIC, MOFCOM, GAC and SAFE on Printing and Issuing Suggestions on Implementation of Several Issues Applicable for Law of Examination, Approval, Registration and Administration of Foreign-funded Enterprises

Gong Shang Wai Qi Zi [2006] No. 81

April 24, 2006

The industry and commerce administrative bureaus, the commerce departments and foreign exchange administrative bureaus of all provinces, autonomous regions, municipalities and cities enjoying the State economic plan, all customs directly under the State Administration of Customs and all national economic and technology development zones:

The "Decisions on Amending the 'Company Law of the People's Republic of China" and the "Decisions of the State Council on Amending the 'Regulations of the People's Republic of China on the Registration Administration of Companies" passed at the 18th session of the Standing Committee of the tenth National People's Congress on 27 October 2005 became effective from 1 January 2006. In order to properly apply the law, implement the examination, approval and registration work of foreign-invested companies in a regulated, convenient and efficient way, promote the sound development of foreign-invested companies and enhance the quality and level of utilizing foreign investment, the "Implementing Opinions on Several Issues Concerning the Application of Law Governing the Examination, Approval and Registration of Foreign-invested Companies" are hereby distributed to you and please implement them accordingly. Should there be any problem in the implementation, please report it timely.

Enclosure: Implementing Opinions on Several Issues Concerning the Application of Law Governing the Examination, Approval and Registration of Foreign-invested Companies

In order to properly apply the law, implement the examination, approval and registration work of foreign-invested companies in a regulated, convenient and efficient way, promote the sound development of foreign-invested companies and enhance the quality and level of utilizing foreign investment, the implementing opinions are hereby provided as follows as to how to apply the Company Law of the People's Republic of China (hereinafter referred to as the "Company Law"), the Regulations of the People's Republic of China on the Administration of Companies Registration (hereinafter referred to as the "Regulations on the Administration of Companies Registration") and the foreign investment related laws, regulations and policies of the State in the examination, approval and registration of foreign-invested companies.

I. The administration of registration of foreign-invested companies shall apply to the Company Law and the Regulations on the Administration of Companies Registration. Where any other law has otherwise been provided in connection with foreign-invested enterprises, such law shall prevail; where no provision has been provided in the Company Law, the Regulations on the Administration of Companies Registration or any law related to foreign-invested enterprises, the administrative regulations or the State Council's decisions relating to foreign-invested enterprises and other foreign investment related provisions of the State shall prevail.

II. Foreign companies, enterprises and other economic organizations or individuals (hereinafter referred to as "foreign investors") may establish a company either in the form of Chinese-foreign equity joint ventures or Chinese-foreign cooperative joint ventures by cooperating with Chinese enterprises and other economic organizations or in the form of foreign joint ventures or wholly foreign-owned enterprises.

Where any foreign investor establishes a one-person limited company in the form of wholly foreign-owned enterprises in accordance with the law, its minimum amount of registered capital shall meet the requirements for one-person limited companies as provided in the Company Law; where any foreign individual establishes a one-person limited company, he shall also confront to the provisions on the foreign investment restriction for one-person limited companies provided in the Company Law. Wholly foreign-owned companies established according to the law prior to 1 January 2006 shall remain unchanged, but the above provisions shall be fulfilled when they alter the registered capital and make foreign investment.

III. The board of directors of a Chinese-foreign equity joint venture and cooperative joint venture is the authority of the company and the organizational structure shall be formulated in the articles of association in accordance with the Law on Chinese-Foreign Equity Joint Ventures, the Law on Chinese-Foreign Cooperative Joint Ventures and the Company Law.

The organizational structure of a foreign joint venture or a wholly foreign-owned company or a foreign-invested stock limited company shall comply with the provisions in the Company Law and the articles of association.

IV. The time limit for applying for the establishment of foreign-invested companies shall comply with the provisions in the Regulations on the Administration of Companies Registration. However, those who establish a company in the form of Chinese-foreign cooperative joint ventures, foreign joint ventures or wholly foreign-owned ventures shall, in accordance with the provisions in the Law on Chinese-Foreign Cooperative Joint Ventures and the Law on Foreign-funded Enterprises, apply for the registration of establishment to the company registration authority within 30 days upon receipt of the approval document. If failed to do so within the limit, the applicant shall confirm the effect of the original approval document with the examination and approval authority or apply for another approval.

V. The qualification certificate or identity certificate of foreign investors submitted to the examination, approval and registration authority when applying for examination and approval and registration of establishment of foreign-invested companies shall notarised by the notary public office of the country and authenticated by the Chinese Embassy (Consulate) in the country. The qualification certificate or identity certificate of investors from Hong Kong, Macau and Taiwan region shall be notarised by the local notary public office.

When applying for examination and approval and registration of establishment of foreign-invested companies, the Power of Attorney in Respect of Service of Legal Documents which is executed by foreign investors (the authorizer) and the domestic receiver of legal documents (the authorized) shall be submitted to the examination, approval and registration authority besides the relevant documents as provided in Article 20 or Article 21 in the Regulations on the Administration of Companies Registration. The Power of Attorney shall indicate the domestic authorized is authorized to receive the legal documents with his address and contact method. The authorized may be a branch established by the foreign investors or a company proposed to be established (if the authorized is a company proposed to be established, the power of attorney becomes effective after the establishment of the company) or other domestic entities or individuals.

Where the company increases its new foreign investors, it shall submit the above documents to the examination, approval and registration authority.

Foreign-invested companies, when applying to the company registration authority for registration of establishment and equity transfer, do not need to submit the equity joint venture contract, the cooperative joint venture contract and credit certificate of investors.

VI. The company registration authority shall, pursuant to the applications, register the type of foreign-invested companies as "limited liability company" or "stock limited company" in accordance with the law and indicate, according to the form of establishment, the words such as "(Chinese-foreign joint venture)", "(Chinese-foreign cooperative joint venture)", "(foreign joint venture)", "(venture wholly owned by foreign legal person)", "(venture wholly owned by foreign non-legal person economic organizations)", "(venture wholly owned by foreign individuals)", "(joint venture between Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau and foreign investors)", "(equity joint venture between Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau and domestic company)", "(cooperative joint venture between Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau and domestic company)", "(joint venture by Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau investors)", "(venture wholly owned by Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau legal person)", "(venture wholly owned by Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau non-legal person economic organization)" and "(venture wholly owned by Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau individuals)" after the "limited liability company", and the words such as "(non-listed Chinese-foreign joint venture)", "(listed Chinese-foreign joint venture)", "(non-listed foreign joint venture)", "(listed foreign joint venture)", "(non-listed joint venture between Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau and foreign investors)", "(listed joint venture between Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau and foreign investors)", "(non-listed equity joint venture between Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau and domestic company)", "(listed equity joint venture between Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau and domestic company)", "(non-listed joint venture by Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau investors)" and "(listed joint venture by Taiwan, Hong Kong or Macau investors)" after the "stock limited company".

The company registration authority may, in accordance with the industrial policies of utilizing foreign investment of the State and relevant provisions, indicate relevant marks such as (investments contributed by the foreign party are less than 25%)", "(mergers of A share)" and "(25% or above of mergers of A share)" after the company type.

For foreign-invested companies established prior to 1 January 2006, the company registration authority shall make relevant adjustment in accordance with the above provisions when going through the alteration registration.

VII. Foreign-invested companies may be engaged in domestic investment after establishment. The company registration authority does not need to issue any relevant qualification certificate of domestic investment.

Where the business license of a foreign-invested company fails to contain the specific type of the company in accordance with Article 6 of these Opinions and the company to be established is a one-person limited company, the company registration authority shall issue a certificate of "venture wholly owned by non-individuals".

VIII. The registered capital of a foreign-invested company may be in Renminbi or in other exchangeable foreign currencies. The conversion between registered capital in foreign currencies and Renminbi or between foreign currencies shall be calculated at the central parity of the exchange rate published by the People's Bank of China on the date of occurrence (payment).

IX. The initial contribution to investment made by the shareholders of a foreign-invested limited liability company (including one-person limited company) shall comply with the laws and administrative regulations. Those who make capital investment in full at one time shall make the payment within 6 months from the date of establishment; for those who make the payment in instalments, the initial capital contribution may not be less than 50% of his subscribed capital contribution or the minimum amount of the registered capital and shall be paid in full within 3 months from the date of establishment, and the rest of the capital contribution shall be paid within the time in accordance with the Company Law, the laws in relation to foreign investment and the Regulations on the Administration of Companies Registration. Where any other law, administrative regulation requires that shareholders shall make the full capital contribution at the time of establishment of the company, such provisions shall prevail.

The capital contribution of a foreign-invested stock limited company shall comply with the Company Law.

X. The ways of capital contribution made by the shareholders of a foreign-invested company shall comply with the provisions in Article 27 of the Company Law, Article 14 of the Regulations on the Administration of Companies Registration and the Provisions on the Administration of Registration of Companies' Registered Capital. Before the State Administration for Industry and Commerce formulates relevant provisions together with relevant departments with respect to the capital contribution made in other properties other than in currency, kind, intellectual properties and land use right, where any shareholder makes capital contribution in other properties other than those as listed in Paragraph 2 Article 14 of the Regulations on the Administration of Companies Registration, his properties shall be evaluated and verified by the appraisal institution established in accordance with the law in China and may not be overvalued or undervalued.

Where the shareholders of a Chinese-foreign equity joint venture with limited liability make the capital contribution in kind (including equipment), industrial properties and other non-monetary properties (excluding land use right) as provided in the Law on Chinese-Foreign Equity Joint Ventures, the price of the contribution may be assessed and determined by each party of the joint venture.

XI. The fund raised by any shareholder of a foreign-invested company in his own name through loan or other ways shall be deemed as his own fund and may be taken as the capital contribution of the shareholder only after it is verified by a capital verification institution.

XII. The time limit for the alteration registration of foreign-invested companies shall comply with the provisions in the Regulations on the Administration of Companies Registration. Where any law or administrative regulation provides or the State Council decides that the companies and their registered matters shall be approved before applying for alteration registration, the companies shall apply for the alteration registration to the company registration authority within 30 days upon approval. If failed to do so within the limit, the applicant shall confirm the effect of the approval document with the examination and approval authority or apply for another approval.

XIII. Foreign-invested companies applying for alteration registration shall submit relevant documents in accordance with the provisions of Articles 27, 29, 31, 32, 33, 34 and 35 of the Regulations on the Administration of Companies Registration. The examination and approval document issued by the original examination and approval authority and the altered approval certificate shall also be submitted if the alteration registration of relevant matters involves the following circumstances:

(I) Registered capital;

(II) Company type;

(III) Business scope;

(IV) Term of operation;

(V) Capital contribution amount subscribed by shareholders or promoters and form of capital contribution;

(VI) Merger or division of foreign-invested companies;

(VII) Alteration of address involving the jurisdiction of more than one examination and approval authority;

(VIII) Equity or stock transfer in limited liability companies or stock limited companies (except for those not involving the matters contained in the business license and approval certificate.)

Except for the circumstances set out above, where the alteration of registered matters of a foreign-invested company involves the amendment to the articles of association, the company shall go through the alteration procedures at the examination and approval authority in accordance with the law within 30 days after going through the alteration registration procedures.

XIV. Where any foreign-invested company relocates (across the jurisdiction of the original company registration authority), it shall go through the relocation procedures at the original company registration authority. If the relocation is across the jurisdictions of the examination and approval authorities, it shall submit an application to the examination and approval authority at the new location. The examination and approval authority at the new location shall, within 5 work days upon receipt of the application, solicit opinions from the original examination and approval authority which shall make a reply within 5 work days upon receipt of the letter of consultation. The examination and approval authority at the new location shall make a reply within 3 work days after receiving the opinions. The original company registration authority shall, within 5 work days upon receipt of the application, solicit opinions from the company registration authority at the new location which shall make a reply within 5 work days. The original company registration authority shall, according to the opinions of granting the relocation of the company from the company registration authority and the examination and approval authority at the new location, take back the business license, issue a relocation certificate and transfer the application materials and the company's registered files to the company registration authority at the new location within 10 work days. The relocating company shall hand in the approval certificate to the original examination and approval authority for cancellation with the relocation certificate and the approval document issued by the examination and approval authority, obtain an approval certificate from the examination and approval authority at the new location, apply for alteration registration to the company registration authority at the new location and obtain a new business license.

XV. Where any foreign-invested company increases its registered capital, the shareholders of limited liability companies (including one-person limited company) and stock limited companies established by way of promotion shall make no less than 20% of the increased registered capital contribution when applying for the alteration registration of registered capital, and the rest of the capital contribution shall be made within the time limit as provided in the Company Law, laws in relation to foreign investment and the Registrations on the Administration of Companies Registration. Where other laws and administrative regulations otherwise provide, such provisions shall prevail.

Where any stock limited company increases its registered capital by issuing new shares, the subscription of new shares by the shareholders shall apply to the relevant provisions on the capital contribution for the establishment of stock limited companies.

XVI. When applying for the alteration of registered capital under the following circumstances, if the imported goods as capital contribution in kind may be exempted from tariff according to relevant provisions, the applicant shall state the relevant facts in writing to the Customs and go through the release (with letter of guarantee) procedures for the imported equipment with the "Confirmation Letter of Domestic and Foreign Invested Projects Encouraged by the State" firstly and then go through relevant procedures for tariff exemption or reduction after the altered company business license is issued:

(I) Where foreign-invested companies apply for capital contribution by imported goods to increase the registered capital, which has been approved by the examination and approval authority;

(II) Where foreign investors or foreign-invested companies acquire any domestic company and in the meantime apply for capital contribution by imported goods to increase the registered capital, which has been approved by the examination and approval authority;

(III) Where foreign-invested companies apply for importing goods due to other alteration of registered capital, which has been approved by the examination and approval authority.

XVII. The foreign exchange administrative department may not require the applicant to provide the altered company business license when handling the following matters:

(I) Where foreign-invested companies apply for altering the foreign exchange registration or opening or altering the capital fund account when increasing the registered capital;

(II) Where foreign investors or foreign-invested companies acquire any domestic company and in the meantime apply for foreign exchange registration or opening capital fund account when increasing the registered capital;

(III) Where foreign-invested companies decrease the registered capital and apply for capital reduction approval to the foreign exchange administrative department;

(IV) Where foreign-invested companies apply for other alterations of foreign exchange registration due to capital alteration.

XVIII. Foreign-invested companies shall go through a filing procedure with the company registration authority if involving the following matters or the alterations:

(I) Amendment draft of the articles of association or amended articles of association not involving the registered matters (including the alteration of total investment), which has been approved by the examination and approval authority;

(II) Directors, supervisors and managers of the company;

(III) Establishment or cancellation of any branch of the company;

(IV) Name list of members and person in charge of the company's liquidation group.

Where the shareholders of foreign-invested companies delay the capital contribution or pay in the registered capital, they shall go through relevant alteration registration in accordance with the Registrations on the Administration of Companies Registration instead of going through a filing procedure.

When handling the filing matters, foreign-invested companies shall submit to the company registration authority a filing report signed by the legal representative (person in charge of the liquidation group) of the company and relevant documents evidencing the matters to be filed. The company registration authority shall handle the filing procedure if the filing documents are complete and issue a filing certificate upon the applicant's request.

XIX. Foreign investors (the authorizer) who alter its domestic receiver of legal documents (the authorized) shall sign a new Power of Attorney in Respect of Service of Legal Documents and file with the company registration authority promptly. The alteration of name, address or other matters of the authorized shall also be filed with the company registration authority promptly. The company registration authority shall record such alterations in the company's files.

Where foreign investors fail to do so and the company registration authority delivers the domestic legal documents to the authorized recorded at the company registration authority, such documents shall be deemed to have delivered to the foreign investors.

XX. Where the shareholders of a foreign-invested company file for equity pledge, they shall submit to the company registration authority an application for equity pledge filing issued by the company, the approval document from the examination and approval authority and the pledge contract. The company registration authority may, after the filing application is accepted, issue a filing certificate upon the applicant's request containing such matters as the name of the shareholders, the proportion of the pledged equity to the total equity of the company, the pledgee's name, the term of the pledge, the examination and approval authority of the pledge contract and so on. During the term of the pledge, the pledger shareholder may not, without the permission of the pledgee, assign or re-pledge the pledged equity or reduce his relevant capital contribution.

XXI. When applying for cancelling the alteration registration in accordance with Article 22 of the Company Law, foreign-invested companies shall submit an application for cancelling the alteration registration and the judgement writ of the court to the company registration authority; if the examination and approval of foreign investment involves, the approval document issued by the examination and approval authority shall also be submitted. If the requirements in the Company Law are fulfilled, the company registration authority shall approve the cancellation of alteration registration and replace a business license if involving the registered matters in the business license.

XXII. After the dissolution causes of a foreign-invested company arise, if the company fails to set up a liquidation group within the limit as provided in the Company Law and the creditors fail to apply to the court for designating a liquidation group, the company's authority, shareholders or creditors may apply to the examination and approval authority for special liquidation in accordance with the Measures on the Liquidation of Foreign-invested Enterprises. For goods under Customs supervision, the company shall go through the Customs procedures and make supplementary payment for relevant taxes.

XXIII. When applying for cancellation of registration, foreign-invested companies shall submit relevant documents in accordance with Article 44 of the Registrations on the Administration of Companies Registration. The liquidation report shall be attached with a cancellation certificate issued by the taxation authority and a certificate from the Customs indicating whether or not the Customs procedures have been completed. Where the foreign-invested company terminates its business activities before the term of operation and applies for cancellation of registration, it shall also submit the approval document issued by the examination and approval authority (except that the company is ordered to dissolve or go bankrupt by court order or is ordered by the administrative authority to close business, revoke the business license or the permit for establishment or the company withdraws its registration of establishment).

XXIV. When establishing or cancelling a branch company, foreign-invested companies may directly apply for registration to the company registration authority in charge of foreign investment at the location of the branch without going through the verification and transfer procedures with the original company registration authority.

Where the establishment and cancellation of a branch company needs to be approved by relevant department in accordance with the laws, administrative regulations, decisions of the State Council or specific provisions of the State concerning the restricted items for foreign investment and in the service trading field, the company shall apply for registration within 30 days from the date of approval. If failed to do so within the limit, the applicant shall confirm the effect of the approval document with the examination and approval authority or apply for another approval.

XXV. The company registration authority is no longer handling the registration of representative offices of foreign-invested companies. Representative offices been registered may not be altered and extended. When the term of representative offices expires, the company shall go through the cancellation of registration or apply for establishing a branch company upon its demand. Branch companies of foreign-invested companies may be engaged in the liaison, consulting or other businesses within the business scope.

In case any company conducts business activities in the name of its representative office, the company registration authority shall investigate and impose a penalty in accordance with the law.

XXVI. Where any shareholder or promoter of foreign-invested companies fails to pay or fails to pay on schedule the monetary or non-monetary properties as his capital contribution, the company registration authority shall impose a penalty on him in accordance with the applicable principles in the Provisions on the Administration of Registration of Companies' Registered Capital. For companies established prior to 1 January 2006, the time for capital contribution shall be equivalent to the time of registration of establishment.

Where any Chinese-foreign cooperative joint venture company fails to perform the capital contribution obligation within the time limit, the company registration authority shall order it to perform the obligation within a specified time in accordance with Article 9 of the Law on Chinese-Foreign Cooperative Joint Ventures; if it still fails to do so, the provisions in Paragraph 1 of the present article shall apply. Where any foreign joint venture or wholly foreign-owned company fails to perform the capital contribution obligation within the time limit, the company registration authority may either handle the case in accordance with the provisions in Paragraph 1 of the present article, or revoke its business license in accordance with Article 9 of the Law on Foreign-funded Enterprises.

XXVII. Where any foreign-invested company goes beyond its approved business scope and is engaged in the business activities under the encouraged or permitted categories of the Guiding Catalogue of Industries for Foreign Investment without permission, the company registration authority shall impose a penalty on it in accordance with Article 73 of the Registrations on the Administration of Companies Registration.

Where any foreign-invested company goes beyond its approved business scope and is engaged in the business activities under the restricted or prohibited categories of the Guiding Catalogue of Industries for Foreign Investment without permission, the company registration authority may determine that it has "conducted illegal business operations by going beyond its approved business scope and without permission being engaged in the business activities which the operator shall obtain the permission or other approval document beforehand" and impose a penalty on the company in accordance with the Measures on Investigating, Punishing and Banning Unlicensed Business Operations. In case a crime is constituted, criminal liabilities shall be pursued in accordance with the law.

XXVIII. For companies established by investors of Taiwan region, Hong Kong SAR, Macau SAR or Chinese nationals with overseas permanent residence (overseas Chinese), and companies established by foreign-invested investment companies or venture capital companies, the administration of examination, approval and registration of such companies shall refer and apply to the present Opinions.
